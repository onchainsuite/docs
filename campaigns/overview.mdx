A campaign is a one-off send to an audience you define: a launch announcement, a governance reminder, a rewards notice. Where [automations](/automation/overview) react to individual events, campaigns go out to a whole cohort at a moment you choose.

## Campaign types

| Type | Channel | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `EMAIL_BLAST` | Email | A single email to the whole audience. The default. |
| `DRIP_CAMPAIGN` | Email | A sequence of steps in order. |
| `SMART_SENDING` | Multi-channel | In-app, Telegram, Discord, X. |

<Warning>
**Only `EMAIL_BLAST` sends today.** Drip campaigns send their first step and stop — later steps and their delays are stored but never dispatched. `SMART_SENDING` campaigns mark themselves as sent without delivering anything.

For sequenced or in-app messaging that works now, use an [automation](/automation/overview) with `wait` and `send_inapp` steps, or send in-app from a campaign via the [in-app path](#in-app-campaigns).
</Warning>

The campaign type is set implicitly by what you configure: adding channels makes it `SMART_SENDING`, adding drip steps makes it `DRIP`.

## Lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Draft">
    Created and editable. Every new campaign starts here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate">
    Checks name, audience, subject, sender, body, and unresolved merge fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scheduled or Sending">
    Launching moves a campaign to `SENDING` and starts dispatch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sent">
    Dispatch finished. Analytics accumulate from here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A campaign can only be launched from `DRAFT` or `SCHEDULED` — relaunching one that's already sending returns a conflict.

<Note>
`ARCHIVED` and `PAUSED` appear as filter options but nothing in the product moves a campaign into them today.
</Note>

## Audience

An audience is a set of contact IDs plus a set of segment IDs. Both are combined — a contact matching either is included.

Segment criteria support three filters: tags, minimum contact score, and whether a wallet address is present.

Before sending, recipients are filtered by:

- **Unsubscribes** — contacts who opted out of email are removed
- **Suppression** — hard bounces and complaints are dropped
- **Smart sending** — if enabled, anyone emailed in the last 10 hours is skipped

Use the audience estimate to see the resulting count, including how many smart sending would exclude.

<Warning>
If a campaign's audience resolves to nobody — an empty segment, or criteria that match nothing — launch does not currently fail cleanly. Always check the estimate before launching, and confirm it's a number you expect.
</Warning>

## Scheduling

<Warning>
**Scheduled campaigns are not sent automatically.** There is no scheduler running. A campaign set to `SCHEDULED` stays there until someone launches it manually.

Treat scheduling as a reminder, not a delivery mechanism — set a calendar alert and launch by hand at the intended time.
</Warning>

When you do schedule, the date you pick is the one that counts. The separate time and timezone fields are recorded alongside it but don't currently affect when the campaign would send.

## Tracking

Open and click tracking are **on by default**; smart sending and UTM parameters are off.

| Setting | Default | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `openTracking` | On | Injects a 1×1 pixel |
| `clickTracking` | On | Rewrites links through a redirect |
| `trackingParameters` | Off | Appends your UTM parameters |
| `smartSending` | Off | Skips anyone emailed in the last 10 hours |

UTM parameters never overwrite ones already present on a link — author intent wins. Links that are anchors, `mailto:`, or `tel:` are left alone.

<Note>
Tracking requires a real per-recipient delivery, so **previews and test sends carry no pixel and no click wrapping**. Test-send opens will never show up in analytics.
</Note>

## In-app campaigns

A campaign can carry an in-app push variant alongside its email, sent to the wallet-reachable part of the same audience. Content resolves from the request, then the campaign's stored in-app content, then a linked in-app template.

Recipients are matched by wallet rather than email, honor a separate in-app opt-out, and skip the email suppression list. Anyone whose message renders empty is skipped rather than failing the send.

See [In-App Notifications](/integrations/in-app-notifications) for how clients receive these.

## Limits

Email and in-app share **one monthly message allowance** — they aren't metered separately.

| Plan | Messages/month | Contacts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pay as you go** | Metered — pay per message | 25,000 |
| **Launch** — $29/mo | 15,000 | 5,000 |
| **Growth** — $199/mo | 100,000 | 100,000 |
| **Pro** — $499/mo | 250,000 | 250,000 |

The allowance is checked at launch against your estimated recipients plus what you've already sent this calendar month, and the send is blocked if it wouldn't fit. Warnings appear at 75% and 95% of the allowance.

On **Pay as you go** there's no monthly cap — sends are metered against your balance instead, so a launch is blocked when the balance won't cover it rather than when an allowance runs out. Top up before a large send.
