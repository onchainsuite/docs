## Before launch

- Validation passes with no errors
- Preview reports no missing merge fields
- A test send rendered correctly in a real email client
- The audience estimate matches what you expect
- Sender identity is verified

## Launching

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.onchainsuite.com/api/v1/campaigns/$ID/launch \
  -H "x-org-id: $ORG_ID"
```

Launch re-runs validation and rejects with `422 VALIDATION_FAILED` if anything is outstanding. It then checks your monthly allowance against the estimated recipients plus what you've already sent this calendar month, returning `402 PLAN_LIMIT_EXCEEDED` if the send wouldn't fit.

Only `DRAFT` and `SCHEDULED` campaigns can launch; anything else returns a conflict.

<Warning>
Scheduling does **not** send. There is no scheduler — a campaign left in `SCHEDULED` stays there indefinitely. Launch manually at the intended time.
</Warning>

Sending is asynchronous: the campaign moves to `SENDING`, is delivered in batches, then moves to `SENT`.

## Metrics

`GET /campaigns/{id}/analytics` returns email and in-app funnels plus a combined total.

### Email

| Metric | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| `sent` | Queued plus sent |
| `delivered` | Confirmed by the provider |
| `uniqueOpens` | Distinct recipients who opened |
| `totalClicks` / `uniqueClicks` | All clicks, and distinct recipients |
| `bounces`, `complaints`, `unsubscribes`, `suppressed`, `failed` | Negative outcomes |
| `deliveryRate` | `delivered ÷ sent` |
| `openRate`, `clickRate`, `unsubscribeRate` | Over `delivered`, falling back to `sent` |
| `clickToOpenRate` | `uniqueClicks ÷ uniqueOpens` |

Rates are percentages rounded to two decimals, and `0` when the denominator is zero.

<Note>
Open and click rates use `delivered` as the denominator **only when delivery confirmations are flowing**. If provider delivery events aren't wired up, `delivered` is zero and the denominator silently falls back to `sent`. Compare `sent` and `delivered` before reading too much into a rate.
</Note>

### In-app

`sent`, `delivered`, `viewed`, `clicked`, `dismissed`, plus `deliveryRate`, `viewRate`, `clickRate`, and `dismissRate`.

In-app has no open pixel — `delivered` is reported by the client when it receives the push, which makes it more reliable than an email open, not less.

## What to watch

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Delivery and bounce trends" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    A rising bounce rate damages sending reputation for every future campaign. Hard bounces are suppressed automatically, but the underlying list-quality problem is yours to fix.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Opens and clicks" icon="chart-line">
    Click-to-open is the honest engagement signal — open tracking is increasingly blocked by mail clients, so open rate alone reads low and noisy.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Unsubscribes" icon="user-minus">
    An unsubscribe sets a flag on the contact that every future campaign honors. Watch the rate per campaign, not just the total — a spike means targeting or frequency, not content.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Onchain response" icon="link">
    The reason to send from a wallet-aware platform. Query [`contact_360`](/api/sql-schema) or `wallet_activity_summary` to see whether recipients actually transacted after the send.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Audit trail

`GET /campaigns/{id}/events` returns the campaign's history — drafted, audience updated, tracking updated, test sent, scheduled, launched, sent — oldest first. This is the fastest way to answer "who changed the audience before this went out".

## Workspace view

`GET /campaigns/analytics/overview?days=30` aggregates across campaigns for a window of 1–90 days, and includes your allowance:

```json
{
  "rangeDays": 30,
  "email": { },
  "inapp": { },
  "totals": { "messagesSent": 12480 },
  "allowance": { "limitKey": "emailsPerMonth", "limit": 25000, "used": 12480, "remaining": 12520 }
}
```

`limit` and `remaining` are `null` on unlimited plans. Note that the overview's `uniqueClicks` is total clicks rather than distinct recipients — use the per-campaign endpoint when the distinction matters.
